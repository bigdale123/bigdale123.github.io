# Introduction
***
I know, I know, another blog post that starts with an introduction section. But my "problem" does warrant a little background. 

On July 14th, I finally got my General class Amateur Radio License. Only a month after my Technician's license, too! To celebrate, I bought my first HF radio to take advantage of my new privileges my license brings. I bought a Xiegu G90, a really cool piece of kit. It's a Software Defined Radio (SDR) that is all mode and all band. It also comes with an internal Antenna Tuner that i've heard is amazing, some say that it would tune dirt if it could. Only real "limitation" is that it's maximum transmit power is 20 Watts. 20W can be plenty in the right conditions, on the [Coastal Waves & Wires](https://www.youtube.com/@COASTALWAVESWIRES) channel on YouTube, Walt (K4OGO) can make some pretty impressive DX contacts on the G90.

My only annoyance was the setup involved to use it. It came with a pelican-style case and foam, but once it was on the table and being used there were wires everywhere. It didn't look as clean as I thought it could be.

As an afterthought, it would probably have been easier (and cheaper) to just buy the Radioddity cooling bracket and live with the cable mess, but where's the fun in that?
