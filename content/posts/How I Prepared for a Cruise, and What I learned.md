This past weekend I went on my first ever cruise. My parents wanted me and my brother to experience A cruise at least once in our life. And I gotta say, cruises are underrated! I had a much more enjoyable time than I thought I would! And depending on where you live, they can be roughly the same cost as a trip to the beach. One thing worried me about going, though, and it wasn't what clothes or how much I should bring:

What was I going to do while on the boat?

So while I will briefly talk about what necessities I packed, such as clothes, I will mainly be focusing on entertainment. Things that I would like to do on the boat. And after I tell you what I took with me, I'll talk about what I learned from the trip. What did I really need? What did I bring too much of?

# The Necessities
---
Now I gotta say, I kinda nailed this part. I only packed a little too much, but still. Better to have it and not need it than to need it and not have it. Two is one and one is none. Better safe than sorry. Take your pick of proverbial phrase.

I had planned for an outfit a day, plus a beach outfit and something to sleep in. The cruise was a 4 day cruise, that started on Thursday and ended with de-embarkation on the following Monday. So that means 5 total, including the clothes I wore on the drive to the boat on Thursday. Friday night dinner on the boat requires "formal" attire, which meant I could just pack a golf shirt and be covered. I also brought sandals which packed pretty flat inside the suitcase I took. For a Hat, I brought a ball cap and a straw hat that I use for yard work. 

In terms of toiletries, The only things you really need to take is probably...
- Toothbrush
- Toothpaste
- Deodorant
- Hair Comb / Brush

You shouldn't need to bring any soaps, if you don't care what soap you use. There is hand soap, shampoo, and body wash provided on the boat. In addition to the necessary toiletries, I took what I need to shave. Definitely did not need to, but I don't like how I look with stubble so I did.

So my total packing list was...

- Mezuzah x2 (one spare)
### Clothes
- 6 Pair Underwear
- 4 Pair Socks
- 4 Shirts
- 2 Tropical Shirts (?)

- 2 Pairs of Shorts
- sun Shirt
- Swim Trunks
- Sleep Shirt
- Sleep Shorts
- Sandals
- Straw Hat
- Ball Cap
### Toiletries
- Toothbrush
- Toothpaste
- Deodorant

You may be wondering, why 6 Pairs of underwear? Please refer to the following proverb:

> Two is one, and one is none.

I think Sun Tzu said that. Jokes aside, it was indeed overkill. But underwear packs really flat and takes up a little space, so an extra pair or two don't hurt.

# Entertainment
---
This is where I spent the most time thinking about what to pack. If I don't pack enough shirts? No big deal, just rotate what you got and wash it when you can. Something I did not know was that (most) cruises have self-serve laundry rooms on board. Not enough to do? I might lose my mind from boredom, I have to feel like I'm doing *something*. And I'm not even saying something productive, either, like getting work done. I just need to be doing something, like watching a movie I haven't seen before or reading a book I haven't read before. And to add another wrinkle, I will not be paying for Internet on the cruise! So everything I plan for entertainment, has to work offline. 

I wanted to bring a diverse amount of things to occupy myself, which I took as an excuse to find new content to consume. I ended up taking new comic books, new books, downloaded some TV shows I wanted to watch, some Movies I wanted to Watch. I loaded up my Retroid Pocket with some games to play (which I hardly even touched because I got sucked into the book I read). In addition, I also brought 2 packs of playing cards and some dice. You never know if you might need them, and they don't take up a ton of space. One thing I was looking for was density, cramming as much as I could in free space on a device. My e-reader was *full*, I had plenty to read. I downloaded Music, TV shows, and Movies to fill up some of the spare space I had on my phone.

The last item of prep was one that was just fun to do. I have recently started hosting a FreshRSS server, to curate blogs I find interesting. I thought to myself, "I won't have any instagram reels, I wonder if there is some way I could create a 'offline' social media substitute". And there is, kinda. It's called Wallabag, a self hosted alternative to pocket that allows you to download articles for offline reading. The week before I left for the cruise, I would send articles to the wallabag server as I read my RSS feed. Before we left I would try to cache the images associated with an article so I could read the articles with images offline. Sadly, in practice, even though I turned on image caching in the Wallabag android app *and* went through each article to load the images so that they would cache, once I got on the boat the images disappeared. But it was still worth doing, having something I can pull up quickly on my phone to fill a couple minutes waiting on something or somebody.

# Realizations
---
- I could have gotten away with fewer clothes. Maybe next time I don't take as many shirts or pants, and wash what I take once I reach the end of what I packed.
	- I could have also counted the clothes I wore on the day we drove to the ship as an outfit, since you can clean clothes on the ship.
- I probably should not have taken the Retroid Pocket.
	- Shocker, I know. It ended up being dead weight, since I only used it once.
	- It takes up *a lot* of space for what little I use it.
	- In the future, maybe I take a gameboy clone or something smaller.
	- I think the main thing I need to do is find games that I *want* to play. When I emulate, I don't spend more than 20 minutes playing a single game. They don't grab me the way they used to...
- I took *way* too much offline content.
	- Not really a downside though. The way I saw it, I essentially just brought a mini library. I had options to choose from.
- I wish I had taken a media player for the TV in the room.
	- Carnival's website says that you shouldn't bring game consoles or media players to use on the TV, because all the input ports are disabled.
	- I am 90% sure this is false, and the reason they say this is because there is no input on the TV remote that they dumb down.
	- When we were fiddling with the TV in the room, we found you can still change inputs using the button *on* the TV.
	- Next time, I will be loading some content on a media player and testing that out.

# Conclusion
---
I *way* over prepared, which isn't necessarily a bad thing. I would rather be over prepared than under prepared. However, I could have left some things at home and still been just as prepared. This would have made keeping track of everything easier, and getting on and off the boat easier.
