This past weekend I went on my first ever cruise. My parents wanted me and my brother to experience A cruise at least once in our life. And I gotta say, cruises are underrated! I had a much more enjoyable time than I thought I would! And depending on where you live, they can be roughly the same cost as a trip to the beach. One thing worried me about going, though, and it wasn't what clothes or how much I should bring:
<br>
What was I going to do while on the boat?
<br>
So while I will briefly talk about what necessities I packed, such as clothes, I will mainly be focusing on entertainment. Things that I would like to do on the boat. And after I tell you what I took with me, I'll talk about what I learned from the trip. What did I really need? What did I bring too much of?

# The Necessities
---
Now I gotta say, I kinda nailed this part. I only packed a little too much, but still. Better to have it and not need it than to need it and not have it. Two is one and one is none. Better safe than sorry. Take your pick of proverbial phrase.

I had planned for an outfit a day, plus a beach outfit and something to sleep in. The cruise was a 4 day cruise, that started on Thursday and ended with de-embarkation on the following Monday. So that means 5 total, including the clothes I wore on the drive to the boat on Thursday. Friday night dinner on the boat requires "formal" attire, which meant I could just pack a golf shirt and be covered. I also brought sandals which packed pretty flat inside the suitcase I took. For a Hat, I brought a ball cap and a straw hat that I use for yard work. 

In terms of toiletries, The only things you really need to take is probably...
- Toothbrush
- Toothpaste
- Deodorant
- Hair Comb / Brush

You shouldn't need to bring any soaps, if you don't care what soap you use. There is hand soap, shampoo, and body wash provided on the boat. In addition to the necessary toiletries, I took what I need to shave. Definitely did not need to, but I don't like how I look with stubble so I did.

You may be wondering, why 6 Pairs of underwear? Please refer to the following proverb:

> Two is one, and one is none.

I think Sun Tzu said that. Jokes aside, it was indeed overkill. But underwear packs really flat and takes up a little space, so an extra pair or two don't hurt.

# Entertainment
---
This is where I spent the most time thinking about what to pack. If I don't pack enough shirts? No big deal, just rotate what you got and wash it when you can. Not enough to do? I might lose my mind from boredom, I have to feel like I'm doing *something*. And I'm not even saying something productive, either, like getting work done. I just need to be doing something, like watching a movie I haven't seen before or reading a book I haven't read before. And to add another wrinkle, I will not be paying for Internet on the cruise! So everything I plan for entertainment, has to work offline.
